Question 1 of 10

In Hosea 8:4, God diagnoses the root of Israel's political and religious disorder. What does he say Israel did regarding kings and princes, and what did they do with their silver and gold?

Question 2 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — Hosea 8:7 uses agricultural imagery to describe the consequence of Israel's idolatry. What two-part picture does the verse give — the proverb about sowing and reaping, and what happens to the standing grain?

Question 3 of 10

In Hosea 9:10, God recalls how he first found Israel using two images of freshness and promise. What are those images, and what did Israel do at Baal-peor that corrupted them?

Question 4 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — In Hosea 9:15, God names a specific location as the turning point of his hatred toward Israel and states a consequence for their leadership. What does the verse say?

Question 5 of 10

Hosea 10:1-2 opens with an image of prosperity turned to guilt. What does the passage say Israel did as his fruit and prosperity increased, and what does that reveal about his heart?

Question 6 of 10

Hosea 11:1-4 shifts from indictment to a tender memory of God's love for Israel as a child. According to the passage, how did God care for Ephraim even as Ephraim kept turning away?

Question 7 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — In Hosea 11:8-9, God wrestles aloud with the prospect of destroying Ephraim as he did Admah and Zeboiim. What reason does he give for holding back his wrath?

Question 8 of 10

Hosea 12:3-4 reaches back into Jacob's own history as a pattern for Israel. What two episodes from Jacob's life does the passage recall, and where does it say God spoke with "us"?

Question 9 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — Hosea 13:14 makes a striking promise about ransom from death, later echoed by Paul in 1 Corinthians 15. What does the verse say, immediately before the note that "compassion is hidden from my eyes"?

Question 10 of 10

Hosea 14:4 gives God's response to Israel's promised return described earlier in the chapter. What does he say he will do, and why?
