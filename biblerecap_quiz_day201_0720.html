Question 1 of 10

In Hosea 1:4, the LORD explains why he chooses the name Jezreel for Hosea's first son. What reason does he give, and what will he do to the kingdom of Israel as a result?

Question 2 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — Immediately after announcing "No Mercy" for Israel in Hosea 1:6, the LORD makes a contrasting promise about Judah in verse 7. What does he say, and how will he accomplish it?

Question 3 of 10

In Hosea 2:8, God exposes Israel's ignorance about the very gifts she was crediting to Baal. What had Israel failed to recognize?

Question 4 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — In Hosea 2:14-15, God pictures alluring Israel into the wilderness to speak tenderly to her. What does he say the Valley of Achor — the very place associated with Achan's sin in Joshua 7 — will become for her?

Question 5 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — In Hosea 3:2, Hosea pays to bring the adulterous woman back to himself. What form did the price take, and what does that detail suggest about her circumstances?

Question 6 of 10

Hosea 3:4-5 predicts a long period of deprivation for Israel followed by a return. According to the text, what will Israel be without during that time, and what follows it?

Question 7 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — In Hosea 4:6, God pronounces judgment on the priests for forgetting the law of God. What consequence does he specifically name in response to that forgetting?

Question 8 of 10

In Hosea 5:12-13, God compares himself to two slow, corrosive forces attacking Israel and Judah. When Ephraim then "saw his sickness," what did he do about it, and how did it turn out?

Question 9 of 10

In Hosea 6:1-3, the people express confidence that the LORD will restore them after affliction. Which pair of images does the passage use for the timing and manner of his coming?

Question 10 of 10 Detail

DETAIL — Hosea 7:8 compares Ephraim to "a cake not turned." In context, what does this image criticize about Israel's condition?
