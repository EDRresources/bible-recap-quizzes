Amos 6-9

Question 1 of 10 Detail

Amos 6:1 pronounces "Woe" on those who are at ease in Zion and feel secure on the mountain of Samaria. How does the verse further identify them?

Question 2 of 10

Amos 6:12 asks two absurd rhetorical questions — "Does one plow the sea with oxen?" — before delivering a verdict on Israel's corruption of justice. What is that verdict?

Question 3 of 10 Detail

Amos 6:13 mocks Israel's boasting over a military victory using wordplay on two place names — one meaning roughly "a thing of nothing," the other "horns." What do the people claim?

Question 4 of 10 Detail

In the first of Amos's visions (7:1-3), locusts devour the land's growth after the king's mowings. How does Amos respond, and what is the LORD's reply?

Question 5 of 10

In the third vision, the LORD stands beside a wall with a plumb line in his hand. What does he say this plumb line means for Israel?

Question 6 of 10

When Amaziah orders Amos to flee to Judah and never again prophesy at Bethel, how does Amos describe his own background and calling in reply?

Question 7 of 10 Detail

In the vision of the basket of summer fruit (Amos 8:1-2), what does the LORD say the fruit signifies for Israel?

Question 8 of 10 Detail

Amos 8:5-6 quotes merchants impatient for the Sabbath to end so they can get back to trading. What specific dishonest practices does the LORD charge them with?

Question 9 of 10 Detail

Amos 9:2-3 pictures Israel's coming judgment as inescapable no matter where they flee. In verse 3, what does the LORD say he will do if Israel hides from his sight at the bottom of the sea?

Question 10 of 10

Amos 9:11 turns from judgment to restoration. What does the LORD promise to do "in that day"?
